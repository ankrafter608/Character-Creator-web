CHARACTER STYLE ANALYSIS

This document analyzes the "Description" field from several high-quality character card examples. The goal is to identify patterns, writing styles, and formatting techniques used by top-tier authors.

### Key Observations

1. **"Show, Don't Tell" via Voice & Perspective**:
   - effective descriptions often adopt a specific voice or perspective rather than a dry list of traits.
   - **Example (Jessica)**: Written entirely in the first person ("I'm Jessica, Jess for short, but you'll call me whatever I feel like..."). This immediately establishes her personality (abrasive, dominant, insecure) without explicitly listing "arrogant" or "rude".
   - **Example (Haruka)**: Uses a narrative summary that contrasts internal thoughts with external actions ("In her mind, these hints are blindingly obvious. In reality... not so much.").

2. **Structured Plain Text**:
   - Usage of Markdown headers (`### Overview`, `### Appearance`, `### Personality`) is common to organize large blocks of text.
   - Bullet points are frequently used for lists (Likes, Dislikes, Habitats).
   - **Plain Text is King**: No complex JSON structures or code blocks within the description field itself. It's readable by humans and parsable by LLMs.

3. **Psychological Depth**:
   - Descriptions go beyond physical appearance. They delve into *motivations*, *fears*, and *internal conflicts*.
   - **Example (Romy)**: Explicitly lists "Internal Agenda", "Fears" (being alone forever), and "Misinterpretation zone" (can seem carefree while feeling deeply). This guides the AI to play a nuanced character.
   - **Example (Rowland Family)**: Provides backstory and relationship dynamics between family members, essential for a multi-character card.

4. **Specific Behavioral Directives**:
   - Instructions on *how* to speak or act.
   - **Example (Romy)**: "Behavioral Directive: Speaks warmly... Tends to address tension quickly... Never manipulates."
   - **Example (Haruka)**: "Defaults to 'yeah,' 'mm,' or silence when anxious."

5. **Contextual & Dynamic Information**:
   - Descriptions often include specific scenarios or "Current Situation" updates to frame the roleplay.
   - **Example (Rose)**: "[Current situation: She has been dating {{user}} for less than a month...]"
